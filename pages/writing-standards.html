Writing Standards

AI-generated text has tells. This checklist prevents them at generation time so you don't have to fix them after.

This is a vocabulary and pattern blacklist, not a voice guide. Apply these rules to all CDE content — blog posts, ad copy, proposals, social media, client reports.

Sentence Patterns to Avoid

Significance Inflation
"marking a pivotal moment" / "a testament to" / "plays a vital role" / "represents a major step forward"

Fix: State what happened. Let the reader decide if it's pivotal.
Copula Avoidance
"serves as" → "is" / "stands as" → "is" / "boasts" → "has" / "features" → "has" or "includes"

Fix: Use simple verbs.
Negative Parallelisms
"Not only X but Y" / "It's not just X, it's Y" / "More than just X, it's Y"

Fix: Just state Y directly.
Synonym Cycling
Calling the same thing by a different name every sentence. If the clearest word is "strategy," say "strategy" three times — don't rotate through "approach," "methodology," "framework."

Structural Patterns to Avoid

Filler Openings
"In today's rapidly evolving..." / "In an era of..." / "As we navigate..." / "When it comes to..."

Fix: Start with the actual point.
Generic Conclusions
"The future looks bright" / "Exciting times lie ahead" / "Only time will tell" / "One thing is clear"

Fix: End with a specific takeaway or action.
Excessive Hedging
"could potentially" / "might arguably" / "it could be said that" / "perhaps one of the most"

Fix: Take a position. If uncertain, say why specifically.
Rule of Three
AI defaults to triplets: "innovation, inspiration, and insights." If only two matter, say two. If four matter, say four. Don't force threes.

The Golden Rule

Write clearly and specifically. Don't add "personality" or "soul" to compensate for removing AI patterns. If you remove filler and what's left is thin — the content needs more substance, not more decoration.
